Exploring the fascinating world of our primate cousins.

What is a Monkey?

Monkeys are primates, a group generally possessing tails and consisting of about 260 known living species. They are highly intelligent, social animals that live in diverse habitats ranging from tropical rainforests to high-altitude mountains.

Common Types of Monkeys

Capuchin

Known as the "organ grinder" monkey, they are incredibly smart and often used in films.

Mandrill

Recognizable by their colorful faces, they are the largest of all monkey species.

Spider Monkey

Found in Central and South America, they have long, spindly limbs and prehensile tails.

Howler Monkey

Famous for their loud howls, they use their vocalizations to communicate across dense forests.

Macaques

The macaques constitute a genus of gregarious Old World monkeys of the subfamily Cercopithecinae. The 23 species of macaques inhabit ranges throughout Asia, North Africa, and Europe. Macaques are principally frugivorous, although their diet also includes seeds, leaves, flowers, and tree bark.

Squirrel Monkey

Small and agile, they are known for their playful behavior and live in large troops.

Tamarin

Small monkeys with distinctive facial hair, often resembling mustaches or beards.

Baboon

Large and terrestrial, baboons are known for their complex social structures and adaptability.

Guenon

Colorful and diverse, guenons are found throughout Africa and are known for their striking facial patterns.

Colobus

Leaf-eating monkeys with distinctive black and white fur, known for their graceful movements through the trees.

Proboscis Monkey

Famous for their large noses, these monkeys are native to the island of Borneo and are excellent swimmers.

Golden Lion Tamarin

Small, brightly colored monkeys native to the Atlantic coastal forests of Brazil, known for their striking golden fur.

Dusky Leaf Monkey

Also known as spectacled langurs, they have distinctive white circles around their eyes and are native to Southeast Asia.

Fun Monkey Facts

  • Tail Talk: Most monkeys have tails, but apes (like Chimps and Gorillas) do not!
  • Social Stars: They use grooming as a way to bond and strengthen relationships.
  • Brain Power: Some monkeys, like Capuchins, use tools like rocks to crack open nuts.
